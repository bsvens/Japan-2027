The Experience

One night in Tokyo. Everything else is the real Japan.

Not a city trip. A route through three completely different versions of Japan — each one built around what you're actually there to feel, not what to check off.

一 ACT ONE Tokyo Mar 5–7 · 2 nights

Land, drop bags, hit the ground. Two nights of Tokyo — one temple icon, an immersive art museum you walk through barefoot, a dawn food crawl through the world's most famous fish market, and the go-kart thing that everyone who's done it says is the most fun they've had in a city. Then you're on a train into the mountains.

Senso-ji Temple · Asakusa

🏎️
MariCAR
Go-kart through Shibuya and Akihabara in costume. One of the most purely fun things you can do in any city on earth — every person who has done it says the same thing.
~$50/person · 2hrs
🌊
teamLab Planets
Barefoot through rooms of projected water, light, and mirrors that react to your movement. Not a museum — an environment you're inside. The group will be talking about it for the rest of the trip.
~$32/person · 1.5hrs
🍣
Tsukiji Market Dawn Crawl
6 AM, fresh tuna, grilled skewers, tamagoyaki, and the best street sushi you've ever had. No tour — just walk in and eat. Best way to start a day in Tokyo.
~$25/person · casual
⛩️
Senso-ji at Dusk
Tokyo's oldest temple in the evening when the crowds thin. Giant red lantern gate, incense, market street alongside it. One sharp impression, then you move on.
Free
二 ACT TWO Hakone Mar 7–9 · 2 nights

The city disappears entirely. Hakone is volcanic mountain terrain — you walk through an active crater, ride a ropeway over sulfur vents, and cruise a lake with Fuji sitting behind it. Then you check into a ryokan on tatami floors and soak in a natural hot spring that comes out of the ground hot. Two different days, completely different energy.

三 ACT THREE Mt. Fuji Mar 9–10 · 1 night

Lake Kawaguchiko puts you at the base of Fuji instead of viewing it from a distance. One night — but the night with the highest ceiling. Paragliding with Fuji as your backdrop is the helicopter moment of this trip: you launch off a hillside, Fuji fills the entire horizon, and you're flying. March still runs weather permitting. If weather closes it, the Fuji 5th Station drive and Chureito Pagoda are legitimate fallbacks that still deliver.

Paragliding · Fuji backdrop Fuji 5th Station Chureito Pagoda Lake Kawaguchi bike
🪂
Paragliding
Launch off a hillside above Kawaguchiko with Fuji filling the horizon directly ahead. Tandem with an instructor, no experience needed. Weather-dependent — operators run when winds allow, which is most days in March.
~$100/person · 30–40min
🏯
Chureito Pagoda
A five-story red pagoda framed in front of Fuji, 398 steps up. Views of the lake and mountain together. The most recognizable Japan shot on earth — accessible year-round and worth the climb.
Free · 30min hike
🚴
Lake Kawaguchi Circuit
Rent bikes and circle the lake with Fuji reflected in the water on calm mornings. Relaxed half-morning before heading to Kyoto — the best way to see the lake at ground level.
~$10/person
🌄
Fuji Panorama Ropeway
Short ropeway up Mt. Kachi Kachi above the lake — Fuji views from 1,075m without the Subaru Line road, which is closed until late April. Operates year-round, 10 min from the station.
~$12/person
四 ACT FOUR Kyoto Mar 10–15 · 5 nights

Five nights with built-in activity density — a wooden boat through river gorge rapids, e-bikes through bamboo and temples, a sunrise hike before crowds arrive, sake brewery tastings in a neighborhood most tourists walk past. Gion in the evenings. And March 14 closes it out right.

🚣
Hozu River Boat
Traditional wooden boat through the Arashiyama gorge, guided through minor rapids by boatmen with poles. 16km of canyon scenery with actual movement — this is the fast-boat equivalent in Kyoto.
~$22/person · 2hrs
🚲
E-bike Temple Circuit
Bamboo grove, Arashiyama, Kinkaku-ji, Philosopher's Path by e-bike. You're moving through the scenery instead of shuffling through it with tour groups. Best way to cover a lot of Kyoto in a day.
~$25/person · half day
🌅
Fushimi Inari at Sunrise
Thousand orange gates up the mountain at 6 AM before anyone else arrives. Golden light, near silence. The photo nobody gets because it requires the early start — which this group will actually do.
Free · early start
🍵
Tea Ceremony
Guided matcha ceremony in a traditional tea house — hands-on, 45 minutes, you're participating not observing. Japan's version of a wine tasting but with centuries of ritual behind it.
~$40/person
🍶
Fushimi Sake District
Canal-lined neighborhood of 40+ breweries, some dating to the 1600s. Afternoon of tastings — multiple varietals, beautiful architecture, almost no tourists. One of the best hidden afternoons in Japan.
~$15/person
🎂
Birthday Kaiseki · Mar 14
Multi-course dinner in Gion for the group. No early flight — 2:45 PM departure on the 15th. This is the night to spend money on dinner and not look at the bill until morning.
~$100–150/person
