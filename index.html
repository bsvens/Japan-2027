The Experience

One night in Tokyo. Everything else is the real Japan.

Not a city trip. A route through three completely different versions of Japan — each one built around what you're actually there to feel, not what to check off.

一 ACT ONE Tokyo Mar 5–7 · 1 night

You land, you drop your bags, and you go see one thing. Tokyo's oldest temple sits in a neighborhood that still moves like it's from another century — a giant red lantern gate, a covered market street leading toward it, incense in the air. That's the impression Tokyo leaves on this trip: a brief, sharp one. Then you're on a train into the mountains.

二 ACT TWO Hakone Mar 7–9 · 2 nights

The city disappears completely. Hakone is mountains, hot springs, and a volcanic lake with Fuji sitting behind it like a postcard you're standing inside. You sleep in a traditional ryokan on tatami floors — dinner is a multi-course kaiseki spread where every dish is its own event. The onsen (a natural outdoor hot spring flowing straight from volcanic ground) is what it sounds like: you sit in water that comes out of the earth hot, in the middle of the mountains, at night. Nothing about it is complicated.

三 ACT THREE Kyoto Mar 9–15 · 6 nights

Six nights because Kyoto earns them. The mornings belong to temples before the crowds arrive — Fushimi Inari's thousand orange gates glow gold at sunrise when almost no one else is there, and you walk up the mountain through them. The afternoons slow to a different pace: a guided tea ceremony in a traditional house (think: wine tasting, Japanese version), then sake tastings in the Fushimi brewery district — a canal-lined neighborhood of century-old breweries most tourists walk past without knowing what they're seeing.

One morning is Arashiyama — the bamboo grove that actually looks like another planet, plus a monkey park on the hill above it with views over the city. Evenings in Gion, Kyoto's old district, are the Europe comparison that actually lands: beautiful architecture, quiet streets, lantern light, extraordinary food. And March 14 is the birthday.
